Open Innovation Challenge

Smart Mattress 2.0

AI & Smart Manufacturing for Enhanced Sleep Quality

About

Company Name: Aaka’s Innovation

Aaka’s Innovation is a fictional manufacturing company focused on developing next-generation consumer products using artificial intelligence (AI) and smart manufacturing technologies. The company aims to transform traditional household products into intelligent, data-driven solutions that improve user comfort, health, and sustainability.

One of Aaka’s Innovation’s core products is Mattress 2.0, an AI-enabled mattress designed to optimize sleep quality through intelligent design, material selection, and advanced manufacturing processes.

Seeker

The Open Innovation Challenge is launched by the Innovation and Advanced Manufacturing Team at Aaka’s Innovation.

The team is responsible for integrating AI, simulation tools, and smart manufacturing systems into product development.

Due to the complexity of combining sleep science, AI modeling, materials engineering, and scalable manufacturing, Aaka’s Innovation is seeking external ideas, technologies, and concepts from innovators worldwide through an open innovation approach.

Theory: What Is Open Innovation by Crowdsourcing

Open innovation is an innovation approach where companies go beyond their internal knowledge and actively involve external actors, such as startups, researchers, students, suppliers, and users, to develop new solutions.

Crowdsourcing is a key mechanism of open innovation. It allows a company to open a specific problem to a broad audience, collect diverse ideas, and identify innovative solutions that may not emerge internally.

By using open innovation through crowdsourcing, companies can:

  • Access multidisciplinary expertise
  • Reduce development time and cost
  • Encourage creative and unconventional solutions
  • Accelerate innovation in complex systems

Aaka’s Innovation adopts this approach to address the challenges of designing and manufacturing an intelligent mattress that meets technical, economic, and sustainability goals.

The Challenge

Background

Traditional mattresses are designed using fixed structures and limited personalization. However, sleep quality is influenced by many factors such as body posture, weight distribution, materials, and individual comfort preferences.

Mattress 2.0 aims to use AI-based simulation and smart manufacturing to improve comfort, adaptability, and sustainability.

The main challenge is to design a mattress system that can:

  • Adapt to different users
  • Be manufactured efficiently at scale
  • Remain affordable and sustainable

Aaka’s Innovation recognizes that solving this challenge requires knowledge from multiple domains and therefore opens this problem to external contributors.

Technical Requirements

  • AI or simulation models for predicting sleep comfort and pressure distribution
  • Smart material combinations or modular mattress layers
  • Data-driven customization methods for different users
  • Manufacturing concepts that support flexible or mass-customized production

Solutions should be technically feasible and compatible with modern manufacturing environments.

Economical Requirements

  • Be cost-effective for large-scale manufacturing
  • Avoid overly complex or expensive components
  • Consider production, logistics, and maintenance costs
  • Demonstrate potential for commercial viability

Affordability and scalability are essential for market adoption.

SDGs Impact

  • SDG 3 – Good Health and Well-Being: Improving sleep quality and physical comfort
  • SDG 9 – Industry, Innovation and Infrastructure: Promoting smart manufacturing and digital innovation
  • SDG 12 – Responsible Consumption and Production: Encouraging efficient material use and reduced waste

Participants are encouraged to highlight the sustainability impact of their solutions.

Award

  • Financial reward of €3,000
  • Opportunity to collaborate with Aaka’s Innovation on a pilot project
  • Recognition through Aaka’s Innovation’s innovation network

Out of Topic

  • Solutions unrelated to mattresses or sleep systems
  • Purely marketing or branding concepts
  • Ideas without a clear technical or manufacturing component
  • Proposals not aligned with sustainability principles
